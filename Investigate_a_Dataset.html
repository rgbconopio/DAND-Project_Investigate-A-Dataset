Introduction¶

Initial Findings¶

There are 10,866 rows and 21 columns in this dataset. We will only keep columns that are relevant in this report and drop the remaining. Features that will be used in this analysis are defined below:

budget: the total budget of the movie in U.S. Dollars\ revenue: the total revenue of the movie in U.S. Dollars\ original_title: the official title of the movie\ genres: a list of genres associated with the movie\ release_date: the month, date, year the movie was released\ vote_count: the number of user votes\ vote_average: the average rating of the movie\ release_year: the year the movie was released\ budget_adj: the total budget of the movie in U.S. Dollars adjusted to account inflation\ revenue_adj: the total revenue of the movie in U.S. Dollars adjusted to account inflation

popularity\ TMDb's documentation on popularity is defined as a measure by user interaction on a certain day. This feature is excluded as it may be biased on recently released movies and may not result in accurate analysis. (https://developers.themoviedb.org/3/getting-started/popularity)

revenue & revenue_adj\ The revenue and revenue_adj columns doesn't appear to have any null values. However, it shows that 6,016 rows have zero revenue. Because we're analyzing the characteristics and features of high-grossing movies, we need data with reported revenue figures.

new column\ This analysis will include movie release dates at the month level. The release_date column will not used directly in this report but will be helpful to extract the release month. Additional column for the release month will be added to the dataset.

duplicate\ One duplicate row will be dropped.

What are features associated with movies that have high revenues?¶

For this question, we'll look into the qualities and characteristics associated in high-grossing movies. The independent variables we're analyzing are budget, genre, and timing of release, while the dependent variable is revenue. The adjusted dollar figures will be used in this analysis to account for inflation over time.

    Conclusions¶

    We looked at three independent variables, amount of budget, timing of release, and genre, to determine the characteristics of movies that have high-revenue. On average, high-revenue movies have high budgets, released on the months of May, June, July, December, and November, and have a combination of Animation, Adventure, Action, Family, Fantasy, and Science Fiction.

    The average viewer rating for all budget categories doesn't have a significant difference, which means higher budget doesn't entirely correspond to higher ratings. However, movies having more than 100 million dollars has lesser variation in ratings because data is more concentrated in the higher end of the observation. High-budget movies have the highest among the lowest ratings in each category.

    Lastly, drama movies has been the most popular genre over the years. Action and thriller movies are identical in numbers until the mid-90's where action movies seemed to slow down. Animation has the steadiest growth among all genres.

    Limitations¶

    The conclusions stated above refers to a sample of the entire population which are movies with reported revenues. To remain consistent on across the board, there are features needed to be omitted for the analysis to be intact. The limitations and assumptions are:

    Revenue: The main dependent variable in this analysis is revenue, therefore, we need data on movies with revenue figures. There are movies in the dataset with zero reported values which can mean two things: the movie did not make any revenue, or no revenue is null. Including these figures would affect the overall analysis and result. These instances will be treated as null values for revenue, hence, will be dropped.

    Budget: Similar instance with revenue, budget figures also have zero-reported figures. Because we dropped the rows having zero revenue, we also eliminated some rows with zero budgets but not everything. The assumption, therefore, will be treated as no budget movies.
